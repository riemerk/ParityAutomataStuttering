\begin{abstract}
In this thesis the stutter-invariant fragment of the linear time $\mu$-calculus $\mutl$ is studied. It is known that the fragment $\mutl(U,R)$ of $\mutl$ without the $\deo$ operator is stutter-invariant. In this thesis we will sketch an automata-based approach of proving this theorem. Firstly we will define a translation of $\mutl$ formulas to Nondeterministic Parity Automata. Secondly we will provide a construction that describes an automaton to recognize the stutter-closure of an automaton. We will use this construction to sketch the proof of the stutter-invariancy of $\mutl(U,R)$. Lastly we will describe the formalization process of the stutter-closure construction in $\lean$.
\end{abstract}

\chapter{From $\mutl$ formulas to automata}
In this chapter we will prove the following theorem, i.e. obtain a translation from a $\mutl$ formula to a \npa. This was the first goal of this thesis.
\setcounter{definition}{15}
\begin{theorem}
 Let $\phi$ be a tidy $\mutl$ formula. Without loss of generality assume $\phi$ is guarded and modal simple. Then there exists an equivalent \npa\, $\A_\phi$ where
 \[
  \Mod_\Psf(\phi) = \LL(\A_\phi)
 \]
\end{theorem}
\setcounter{definition}{0}

\noindent We will obtain this translation with different steps. We will first translate a $\mutl$ formula to a parity formula, then this parity formula to an \apa\ and finally this \apa\ to an \npa. This chapter is structured as follows: In Section \ref{section:mutltoparform} we will define the translation of any tidy $\mutl$ formula to a parity formula. In order to translate this parity formula to an \apa\ we need it to be simple guarded, an extra strong version of guardedness which we will define in Section \ref{section:guardednessandsimple}. In Section \ref{section:parformtoapa} we will translate a simple guarded \apa. In section \ref{section:APAtoNPA} we will provide a translation from an \apa\ to a \npa\  and finally in section \ref{section:mutltoNPA} we will use all the introduced theorems to prove the Theorem.

\chapter{Determining the stutter-invariant fragment of $\mutl$}
In this chapter we will obtain a method to prove for an arbitrary $\phi\in\mutl$ whether it's language is stutter-invariant or not and we will give a sketch of the proof that $\mutl(U,R)$ is the stutter-invariant fragment of $\mutl$. In Section \ref{section:stutterclosedautomata} we will describe a construction to determine the stutter-closure of the language of an automaton. We use this construction to check if the language of an automaton is stutter-invariant. In Section \ref{section:mutlstutinvariant} we use this check on the language of an automaton to prove that certain $\mutl$ formulas are and some are not stutter invariant and lastly we give the first ideas for a proof that $\mutl(U,R)$ is the stutter-invariant fragment of $\mutl$.
